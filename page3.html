Senior Web Developer

Job Summary

This job position is usually held by a person that has 5 - 10 years experience in the field of web development, a Senior Web Developer is someone who can manage both the back (Web Server Management) and front (HTML,CSS,JavaScript) end of a Website . They also are able to lead a project, being able to answer questions from colleagues and clients alike.

Job Requirements

The position usually requires you to have a diploma in a relevant field for example General Computing or Computer Science. On the website, Reed, You can see the variance in requirements needed for the job role but some of the most consistant ones are Ability to work as part of a team and good people skills for contacting clients. More technical skills involve the knowledge of JQuery, HTML, CSS, AngularJS and BootStrap. Also being comfortable with GitHub is sought after.

Salary

The average salary for a job in this field ranges from £35,000 to £80,000 depending on which part of the country you live (closer to London means more money).

Full Stack Developer

Job Summary

A Full Stack Developer is a job title that refers to an individual that is competent in a wide range of programming/coding languages across the full spectrum of computing, they are not required to be a full expert in one specific area. These types of jobs are usually required when a project is in the planning phase, a Full Stack Developer would be introduced so they can understand all stages of the project.

Job Requirements

The postion os a Full Stack Developer is required to have a broad understanding of most of the programming/coding languages used to create a specifc project, these may include C#, C++, Python etc. A Full Stack Developer would also have an understanding of both the Front and Backend of the project and also maybe required to do mockups of the project for presentations. This postions usually requires a degree in Computer Science/Computing aswell.

Salary

According to Indeed, the annual salary of a Full Stack Developer differs depending on the range of langauges known and the amount of experience but salraries start at £25,000 and go up to around £60,000.

Junior Web Developer

Job Summary

Surprisingly, the job position of Junior Web Developer does not require much more knowledge than what we have learnt in out first term at University of Northampton. A Junior Web Developer will spend most of the time building the front end of a website, with a small interaction with the backend of the project.

Job Requirements

Job requirements again vary but consistent requirements include, enough knowledge to create a website from scratch, ability to edit and resolve issues with existing websites, Ability to work to W3C standard and ability to work to a deadline.

Salary

As you can see here on Indeed the salary varies from £19,000 to £25,000, some places offer a hourly wage rather than salary (presumably for temporary jobs).
